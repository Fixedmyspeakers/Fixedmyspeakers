How It Works 🛠

FixMySpeaker.com is a cutting-edge, free online tool specifically engineered to rejuvenate your phone’s speaker by effectively removing trapped water, accumulated dust, and microscopic debris using precisely generated sound waves. This innovative method leverages the principles of acoustic vibration to dislodge foreign particles without physical intervention, ensuring a safe and non-invasive cleaning process.

When you play specific high-frequency audio tones through our tool, your phone's speaker vibrates intensely. These rapid vibrations create a powerful outward force that literally "shakes out" water droplets and pushes away dust particles that have settled within the speaker grill and internal components. This process is particularly effective for accidental spills, rain exposure, or even just long-term dust buildup that can significantly muffle sound quality. With no app download or installation required, FixedMySpeaker.com offers an unparalleled, easy, and effective solution for immediate speaker restoration, trusted by thousands globally.

Can it repair physical speaker damage?▶️
No, this tool is designed to clear blockages from water or dust. It cannot repair physical hardware damage, such as a torn speaker membrane, a faulty coil, or disconnected wires. If your speaker issues persist after cleaning, we recommend consulting a professional phone repair technician.

How to prepare my phone before cleaning?▶️
Before starting the cleaning process, make sure your phone's volume is set to maximum. Point the speaker downwards to allow water to drip out naturally. You can also gently tap the phone on a soft surface or shake it lightly to aid the water expulsion.

Is there a specific frequency I should use?▶️
The default frequency of 440 Hz is a good starting point and often effective. However, you can experiment with the frequency slider between 100 Hz and 1000 Hz. Different frequencies might work better for different types of blockages or speaker designs. Listen for changes in the sound as you adjust.
